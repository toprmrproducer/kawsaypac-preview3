More Than a Name

Kawsay means lifeKawsaypac means cheers to life

In Quechua, one of the oldest Indigenous languages spoken throughout the Andes, Kawsay means life and Kawsaypac means cheers to life. These are words that speak not only to being alive, but to living with vitality, purpose, gratitude, and harmony. When we first learned their meaning, it felt as though it had been waiting for us all along.

As two South Americans, one from Bolivia and one from Ecuador, this name carries a special significance. It reflects our ancestral roots, our journey back to the land that shaped our families, and our commitment to honoring the traditions that have cared for these communities long before modern wellness existed.

A Journey That Changed Everything

Our search for medicinal plants didn't begin because we wanted to create an herbal line. It began because we wanted to better understand health. After years of navigating our own wellness journeys, we found ourselves asking a simple question: How did people care for themselves before pharmacies existed?

That question led us deep into the Ecuadorian Andes and Amazon, where we spent time with Kichwa communities, traditional healers, and elders whose relationship with medicinal plants has been cultivated over countless generations. We arrived with curiosity, listened with humility, and left with an entirely new understanding of what it means to work with nature instead of against it.

Every Herb Has a Story

Every herb has a story.

The herbs in our Kawsaypac collection are not selected because they're trending or because they make impressive marketing claims. They're here because they've earned the trust of the communities who have relied on them for centuries.

Each plant carries a history of observation, respect, and lived experience. That knowledge continues to inspire every formula we create. While we embrace modern research, we also recognize the immense value of ancestral wisdom that has stood the test of time.

Rooted in Reciprocity

One of the greatest lessons Ecuador has taught us is that healing begins with relationship. Relationship with the land. Relationship with the people who protect it. Relationship with the plants themselves.

That's why we source our herbs directly from Kichwa communities and trusted local harvesters rather than through anonymous suppliers or middlemen. Every herb is sustainably wild-harvested with deep respect for Pachamama, ensuring the forests are protected, the traditions are honored, and the families preserving this knowledge are directly supported.

For us, ethical sourcing isn't simply part of our business model. It's a responsibility.

We don't believe you have to choose between ancestral knowledge and modern science. We believe the two can exist beautifully together.

Why the Flower of Life?

Why the Flower of Life?

The Flower of Life has long been recognized as a symbol of connection, creation, and the intricate patterns woven throughout nature. Every circle touches another, reminding us that nothing exists in isolation. Our bodies, our emotions, our communities, and the natural world are all part of something greater.

That philosophy lives at the heart of Kawsaypac. Every herb begins in healthy soil, is gathered with intention, shared through trusted hands, and eventually becomes part of someone else's healing journey. The Flower of Life reminds us that every step in that process matters, and that wellness is never created by one thing alone, but through the relationships that sustain it.

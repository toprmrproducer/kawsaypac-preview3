Sacred Sacral: The Complete Guide to Our Womb Wellness Blend

June 20, 2026 · 3 min read · By the Kawsaypac team

In every Ecuadorian community we source from, there is a body of plant knowledge held and passed between women: which herbs for the cycle, which for the years of change, which warm cup for the low dragging days. It is practical, unsentimental knowledge, older than any wellness industry. Sacred Sacral is our blend built in respect of that tradition, and this guide walks through what it is, how to prepare it, and who tends to love it.

What Sacred Sacral is

Sacred Sacral is a warming botanical blend centered on the herbs traditionally used in womb and hormone-supportive rituals: soft leaves of the raspberry-leaf type, petals, and gently warming barks, in whole form. As with all Kawsaypac blends, the formula breathes a little with each harvest, so the pouch in your hands lists the exact current composition. Read it, and if you are pregnant, trying to conceive, nursing, or on hormonal medication, bring that list to your midwife or clinician before starting. That is not fine print. Herbs in this category are precisely the ones that deserve a professional look first.

The tradition behind it

The women's herb tradition in Ecuador is domestic, not clinical. Warm infusions appear at specific moments: in the days before and during menstruation, in postpartum warming practices, in the daily rhythm of women doing hard physical work at altitude. Warmth itself is treated as a therapeutic ingredient, which is why these are hot, slow teas rather than capsules. When our partner families talk about these plants, the framing is maintenance and companionship through the body's cycles, not correction of a problem. We think that framing is worth importing along with the herbs.

How to prepare it

Brew Sacred Sacral generously: a heaping tablespoon per large cup, water just off the boil, covered, seven to ten minutes. It pours a blushing amber with a rounded, faintly fruity softness from the petals and leaf. Many of our customers time it to their cycle, drinking it daily in the week before menstruation and through it, then more casually the rest of the month. Others simply make it a fixed evening cup, where its warmth pairs naturally with the wind-down ritual we describe in our deep sleep guide. There is no single correct cadence. Consistency at whatever cadence you choose is what the tradition would recognize.

A practical note: this blend is lovely with a little raw honey, and it takes a splash of oat milk better than most herbals. Ritual should taste good. That is how rituals survive.

Who it is for

Sacred Sacral is for women who want a plant-based ritual attached to their cycle rather than a supplement regimen: the person whose premenstrual days feel cold and heavy, who carries tension low in the body, or who is rebuilding a relationship with her rhythm after years of ignoring it. It is also a frequent gift purchase, we have noticed, from women to their daughters and sisters, which fits exactly how this knowledge has always moved. If your interest is more general calm than cycle support, Scales of Balance may be the better first pouch.

Why ours is different

Herbs marketed to women are some of the most cynically sold products in American wellness: pink labels over anonymous commodity powder. We wanted the opposite, and built it. The botanicals in Sacred Sacral are wildcrafted or forest-garden grown by Indigenous partner families in Ecuador, hand-harvested in season, dried whole, and imported in small traceable batches to be blended and shipped across the US. The women who harvest these plants use these plants. That closed loop, from a chakra garden in Ecuador to your kitchen in America with nothing anonymous in between, is what Kawsaypac exists to provide, and in this category it matters more than in any other.

Common questions

Can I drink it through my whole cycle? Many customers do, gently, with a heavier emphasis in the premenstrual week. Bodies differ, and this is exactly the blend to introduce gradually while you watch your own response. Anyone on hormonal medication or with a diagnosed condition should review the pouch list with a clinician first; we repeat that on purpose.

Is it only for women? The tradition it draws from is a women's tradition, and that is who we built it for. That said, it is a warming, pleasant botanical tea, and nobody is checking at the kettle.

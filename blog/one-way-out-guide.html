One Way Out: The Complete Guide to Our Elimination Blend

June 27, 2026 · 3 min read · By the Kawsaypac team

The name is not subtle, and neither is the problem it addresses. Everyone's digestion occasionally stalls: travel, stress, a week of eating like a raccoon at a festival. One Way Out is our focused elimination-support blend for exactly those moments, a clear-purpose tool with a defined job and a short working window. This guide explains where it sits in our digestive lineup, how to use it with intelligence, and when to choose something else entirely.

What One Way Out is

One Way Out is a targeted blend from the traditional moving-herb families, leaves and barks and seeds with a well-earned reputation for encouraging motility, prepared whole as always and formulated for occasional, situational use. Think of our digestive range as three gears: Bowel Balance is the everyday gear, gentle enough for months of daily rhythm. Bowel Banisher is the deep-cleanse course for a committed reset window. One Way Out sits between and beside them: the situational gear, the pouch you open when the system has stalled and needs a confident, short nudge back into motion. Your pouch prints the exact current formulation and its directions, and with movers, directions are the product. Follow them.

The tradition behind it

Every herbal tradition on earth keeps moving plants, and every tradition ringfences them the same way: for occasions, not for always. In the Ecuadorian communities we work with, stronger digestive herbs come out after heavy festival seasons, during travel, at moments of obvious stagnation, and then they go back on the shelf while the daily gentle cups resume. The wisdom is in the boundary. Bodies adapt to constant stimulation, which is why tradition treats movers as punctuation rather than prose. We named this blend the way we did partly so nobody mistakes it for an everyday tea.

How to use it

Brew a covered infusion per the pouch directions, typically a tablespoon, water just off the boil, seven to ten minutes. Evening is the traditional slot, taken after your last meal so the blend works with the body's overnight rhythm toward a calm, unhurried morning rather than an ambush at your desk. Start with a single cup and give it a full cycle before judging; the point is a return to natural rhythm, not fireworks. Pair it, always, with the mechanical basics that make any mover's job easier: real water through the day, a walk after dinner, and the earlier-last-meal habit from our sluggish digestion guide. Use it for a few days to a week, and as rhythm returns, hand the job back to a gentle daily blend.

Who it is for, and who it is not

One Way Out is for the traveler whose gut stayed home, the festival survivor, the stress-week casualty, anyone functionally regular whose system occasionally parks itself. It is not for chronic constipation, which deserves a clinician's attention rather than an escalating tea habit; not for pregnancy or nursing; not for anyone with bowel conditions or medication-timing concerns without professional sign-off; and not for daily indefinite use, which the name, we hope, already discouraged. If you find yourself needing it weekly, that is your body requesting the full protocol conversation, not a bigger pouch.

Why ours is different

Occasional-use herbs are where cut corners hide best, since customers meet them rarely and judge them loosely. We hold the line anyway: One Way Out is whole Ecuadorian botanicals, wildcrafted or forest-garden grown by Indigenous partner families, harvested in season, traceable to lot, imported small-batch and shipped fresh across America with directions we actually want you to read. A blend you reach for a few times a year should work the first time, gently and reliably, and that reliability is grown and handled into the material long before it reaches your kettle. That is the Kawsaypac standard, applied without exception to the pouch you will use least and appreciate most.

Common questions

How fast does it work? Typically with the body's overnight rhythm after an evening cup, which is exactly the pace you want: an unhurried morning, not an emergency. If nothing has shifted after two or three evenings, stop escalating and revisit water, movement, and meal timing, or step up deliberately to a proper course of a stronger blend.

Is it habit-forming? Used as directed, for occasions, no; that boundary is why the directions read the way they do. The habit-forming pattern is daily reliance on any mover, which is why we built our range with a gentle daily blend at its center and this pouch explicitly at its edge.
